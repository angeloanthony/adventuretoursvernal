Backcountry Tours in Vernal, Utah

Updated April 2026 · Adventure Tours Vernal

The real Vernal isn't on the highway — it's in the backcountry. Remote canyons, thousand-year-old petroglyphs, outlaw hideouts, hidden arches, and terrain that looks like it hasn't changed since the Jurassic. A guided backcountry UTV tour is the only way most people will ever see it.

What "Backcountry" Means Here

Vernal's backcountry isn't a marketing term — it's literal. The trail systems operated by Adventure Tours Vernal run through terrain with no cell service, no signage, no other vehicles, and no way in without knowing the route. These are canyon corridors, desert washes, ridge-top traverses, and historic paths that have been used for centuries but never developed. That's what makes them special, and that's why you need a guide.

Five Trail Systems, Five Experiences

Doc's Beach: Open sandy terrain along the Green River corridor. Fast, wide, and accessible. The most popular trail for first-timers who want the backcountry feeling without technical demands.

Moonshine Arch: Red rock canyons winding to a hidden natural sandstone arch. Beautiful terrain with a destination worth the ride. Great for photographers.

Ashley Gorge: Towering sandstone walls create a dramatic canyon corridor with river valley scenery. The most visually immersive trail.

Outlaw Trail: The most remote and rugged option. Follows historic routes used by Butch Cassidy and the Wild Bunch through canyon terrain that feels genuinely untouched. For guests who want the deepest backcountry experience.

Asphalt Ridge: Ridge-top panoramas with 360-degree views of Dinosaur Country and the Uinta Mountains. A different perspective on the basin — elevation instead of canyons.

Your Guides: Dave & Trudy Wilson

Every tour is personally guided by Dave and Trudy Wilson, who built the trail routes and know every rock art panel, every overlook, and every piece of history along the way. They each ride their own Kawasaki KRX 1000 alongside your group, adjusting pace to match your skill level and interests. This isn't a follow-the-leader caravan — it's a personal guided experience through terrain they've spent years exploring.

Equipment & What's Included

Each guest machine is a Kawasaki KRX 1000 2-seater with FOX 2.5 PODIUM LSC suspension, full roll cage, power steering, and automatic transmission. Helmets and safety gear are provided. A thorough safety briefing covers vehicle operation before departure. No prior off-road experience is required.

Tours are three hours at $299 per machine (up to 2 riders). The $100 ride-along option lets a third person ride as a passenger with Dave or Trudy. Groups up to 12 guests across 6 machines — call for custom group pricing.

Backcountry Tip: If you want the most remote, rugged experience, ask for Outlaw Trail when you book. If you want drama and beauty with a hidden destination, go with Moonshine Arch. Dave will help you match the trail to your group.
