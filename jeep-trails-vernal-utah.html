Jeep Trails Near Vernal, Utah

Updated April 2026 · Adventure Tours Vernal

Vernal sits in the middle of some of Utah's best off-road terrain — the same canyon country, desert washes, and backcountry routes that make Moab famous, minus the crowds and the premium pricing. Whether you're bringing your own Jeep or want to experience the trails in a guided UTV, here's what's out there.

Off-Road Terrain Around Vernal

The Uintah Basin is ringed by BLM land with a network of unpaved roads and trails ranging from graded gravel to technical rocky routes. The terrain south and east of town — toward Dinosaur National Monument and the Book Cliffs — is where the most dramatic canyon and mesa country starts. Red rock formations, deep washes, slickrock shelves, and remote mesa-top roads give Jeep and 4x4 drivers plenty of variety.

North of town, the roads climbing into Ashley National Forest and the Uinta Mountains offer a different character — forest service roads through aspen and pine with mountain views and seasonal creek crossings. The Diamond Mountain area is popular for Jeep camping and overlanding.

Guided UTV Tours — No Jeep Required

If you don't have a 4x4 or want access to trails that require local knowledge to navigate, Adventure Tours Vernal runs guided Kawasaki KRX 1000 tours through five trail systems that cover the best of the Vernal backcountry. The KRX 1000 handles terrain that would challenge most stock Jeeps — sandy washes, rocky climbs, and narrow canyon routes — and Dave and Trudy Wilson know every turn, every petroglyph panel, and every overlook.

Many Jeep enthusiasts book a guided UTV tour as a scouting mission — they ride with Dave first, learn the area, then come back with their own rigs and explore further. It's a smart approach for a region where the best trails aren't marked and GPS coverage is spotty.

Vernal vs. Moab for Off-Roading

Moab has the name recognition, but Vernal delivers the same caliber of terrain at a fraction of the cost and congestion. You won't wait in line for trails here. You won't fight for a campsite. And the landscape — canyon walls, natural arches, petroglyphs, and outlaw history — is just as compelling. The main trade-off is fewer services; Vernal doesn't have the same rental infrastructure as Moab, which is why guided tours fill that gap so effectively.

Planning Your Off-Road Trip

If you're bringing your own Jeep, the BLM Vernal Field Office can provide current maps and route conditions. Cell service drops out quickly once you leave pavement, so download offline maps before heading out. Fuel up in town — there's nothing once you're in the backcountry. Spring and fall are the best seasons; summer is rideable but hot, and some routes close during winter snow and mud season.

Jeep Owner Tip: Book a guided UTV tour on day one to scout the area. Dave can point you toward the best Jeep-accessible routes based on your rig's capability and what you want to see. It's the fastest way to learn a new off-road area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there Jeep trails near Vernal?

Yes — extensive BLM backcountry routes plus five guided UTV trail systems. Terrain ranges from graded gravel to technical canyon and mesa routes.

Can I do a guided tour instead of driving my Jeep?

Yes. Adventure Tours Vernal provides KRX 1000 machines, guides, and gear for $299/machine for 3 hours across five trail systems.

How does Vernal compare to Moab?

Similar terrain quality, significantly fewer crowds, and competitive pricing. Less rental infrastructure, which is why guided tours are the way to go.
